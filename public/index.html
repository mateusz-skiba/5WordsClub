    Example The atmosphere at the concert was amazing.

    Forms atmospheres

    Common uses create an atmosphere, atmosphere of excitement

    concentrate

    To focus your mind or attention on something.

    Example She needs to concentrate on her homework.

    Forms concentrates, concentrated, concentrating

    Common uses concentrate on, concentrate hard

    particular

    Used to talk about one specific thing or person, not general.

    Example I don’t have a particular plan for the weekend.

    Forms particularly

    Common uses in particular, particular attention

    luxury

    Example They live in a house with many luxuries.

    Forms luxuries

    Common uses luxury goods, live in luxury

    interview

    Example She has an interview for a new job tomorrow.

    Forms interviews, interviewed, interviewing

    Common uses job interview, interview for a position

    mountain

    A very big hill, much taller than a regular hill.

    Example We climbed a tall mountain last summer.

    Common uses climb a mountain, big mountain

    listen

    To hear something and pay attention to it.

    Example I like to listen to music.

    Common uses listen to music, listen carefully

    book

    A set of pages with stories, facts, or pictures.

    Example She is reading a book about animals.

    Common uses read a book, buy a book

    travel

    To go from one place to another.

    Example I want to travel to Paris next year.

    Forms luxuries

    Common uses travel by plane, travel abroad

    friend

    A person you like and spend time with.

    Example My best friend is very kind.

    Forms interviews, interviewed, interviewing

    Common uses best friend, close friend

    Story

    My friend and I are planning a travel adventure to a mountain region, and I'm reading a book about it to prepare. We'll listen to local guides and learn as much as we can.

    detrimental

    Something that causes harm or damage.

    Example Something that causes harm or damage.

    Forms detriment

    Common uses detrimental effect, detrimental impact

    profound

    Having deep meaning or great significance; often used to describe deep thoughts or feelings.

    Example The speech had a profound impact on the audience.

    Forms profundity

    Common uses profound impact, profound thought

    redundant

    Not necessary because something similar already exists or has been done.

    Example The extra details were redundant and didn't add value.

    Forms redundancy

    Common uses redundant information, redundant task

    articulate

    Able to express thoughts clearly and effectively in speech or writing.

    Example Able to express thoughts clearly and effectively in speech or writing.

    Forms articulation, articulately

    Common uses articulate speaker, articulate argument

    vehemence

    The strong feeling or force behind something, especially in a way that shows anger or passion.

    Example Her vehemence in defending her point of view was clear to everyone in the room.

    Forms vehement

    Common uses vehemence of opinion, vehemence of protest

    Story

    Speaking with such vehemence about the issue felt detrimental to the discussion, making some points seem redundant. While his points were articulate, the emotional charge obscured the profound message he was trying to convey.
